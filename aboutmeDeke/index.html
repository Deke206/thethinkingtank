What I am doing right now

Uber delivery was my last job. I am working toward something more stable.

Before the pandemic, I worked around Airbnb and property operations. When that work disappeared, I shifted into Uber deliveries and kept doing it for nearly five years.

The app helped me survive, but the income became too unpredictable. The platform controls the rates and access to the work, while I still carry the repair costs, batteries, transportation and physical wear. I am not trying to avoid work—I am trying to replace unstable app income with direct customers and useful paid projects.

    What you may see from the road

    I am usually working on the e-bike, wiring, parts or a project on my computer.

    The e-bike is how I have handled deliveries, so I have had to track down parts, test connectors and repair wiring to keep it moving. At other times I am on the PC building websites, researching products, planning LED systems or trying to turn an idea into paid work.

    The van is where my tools and computer are, so it doubles as my workspace. The LED banner is there to turn a few seconds at a stoplight into a possible customer, referral or useful connection.

    Why cars and vans

    I love racing, cars and working with my hands.

    The Cannonball Run helped spark my love of vans and the idea of driving across the country. That interest grew into years around driving, vehicles, customer service and figuring out mechanical or electrical problems.

    Work I can do

    A small real job is more useful than a big promise.

    AI is one tool I use. The experience, direction, ideas and effort are mine.

    Background in brief

    I have been responsible for the whole job before.

    I previously owned and operated a limo and town-car business, which meant handling customers, scheduling, sales, presentation, marketing and vehicle problems. I also have experience around property management, Airbnb operations and an automotive business. I learned Photoshop, Illustrator, web work and technical troubleshooting because business owners often have to solve the problem themselves.
